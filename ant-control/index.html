Ant trails are essentially the circulatory system of an ant colony's foraging efforts. Worker ants use pheromones, chemical scents, to mark trails from the nest to food or water sources. Disrupting these trails is a critical step in ant control, but simply wiping them away is rarely enough, as the ants will just re-establish the trail or find a new route. Professional treatments target the underlying attractants and the colony itself, breaking the cycle of foraging and trail formation. Our technicians understand the science behind ant trails and utilize this knowledge to apply treatments in a way that maximizes contact with foraging ants and ensures the product is effectively transferred back to the colony.
